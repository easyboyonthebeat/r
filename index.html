SPEAK UP AGAINST CHILD ABUSE

Child abuse happens when a child is harmed physically, emotionally, sexually, or when a child’s basic needs such as food, care, protection, and education are neglected. Sadly, many children experience abuse in silence because they are afraid or do not know who to tell. Child abuse can show itself in different ways. A child who is being abused may suddenly become withdrawn, fearful, aggressive, or may have unexplained injuries. Some children may lose interest in school or become unusually quiet and sad. These signs should never be ignored. This is why the message “Speak Up Against Child Abuse” is very important. When we speak up, we protect children. When we report abuse, we help stop the harm and give the child a chance to receive help and support. Students should remember that no one has the right to hurt you. If anyone touches you inappropriately, threatens you, or makes you feel unsafe, you must speak to a trusted adult such as a parent, teacher, school counselor, or guardian. Teachers, parents, and community members also have a responsibility to listen to children, believe them, and take action when abuse is suspected. A safe child today will grow into a confident and responsible adult tomorrow. Protecting children is everyone’s duty. Let us therefore work together to create safe homes, safe schools, and safe communities where every child can grow in love, dignity, and security. Remember: If you see something wrong, say something. Speak up against child abuse. Thank you.
